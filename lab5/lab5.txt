Question 6:
a. The Beale type is a struct that contains the letters in the cipher and a separate field to keep a count of 
how many letters. encipher() and decipher(), on the other hand, just return an int array and a char array with 
no separate length. Is one approach better than the other, or are they essentially the same?

The Beale struct with the length field is more reliable because it keeps the data and its size together. 
encipher() and decipher() rely on sentinel values (the int* array ends with a special number, 
and the char* array ends with \0) to mark the end. If a sentinel value ever appeared as real data by accident, 
it could cause problems. Keeping the length stored separately makes the program clearer and less error-prone.

c. What is your opinion on the three ciphers (Caesar, Vigenère, Beale) we have looked at?
From the three cipher methods we have seen, they have increased in complexity and security with each one. Caesar ciphers being the weakest, 
Vigenere being of moderate complexity and security, and Beale being the most complex we have seen. 
These ciphers are extremely unsecure by today's standards but are very interesting to see. 
In their times, these would have been ingenuative ways to keep messages and information secure. 
They are also interesting in the fact they could possibly be the base for many modern ciphers and encryption methods.
