1. To 'undo' the Caesar cipher, the recipient would have to be given the 'key' (the number used to rotate the letters). 
The person looking to read the message would then have to know in which direction the letters were rotated or try both directions. 
If the reader did not have the key, they would need to rotate the letters in the message through all possible combinations until there 
are words that are legible. Depending on the length of the message, it could take a while but nothing susbstantial compared to other methods
of encryption.
